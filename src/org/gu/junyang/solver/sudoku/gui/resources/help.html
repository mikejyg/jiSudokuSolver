Definitions

cell set: is each of the row, column and the 9-cell square of the Sudoku puzzle.

candidate: is a possible value a cell can have, when the value of the cell is not know.

candidate list: is the list of candidates of a cell, when the value of the cell is not know.


Solve Methods

level 1: Eliminate candidate, with single candidate conversion
For each known cell, eliminate candidates of other cells within the same set, and convert cells with a single candidate as known.

level 2: Single Destination
Within a cell set, if a candidate has only one destination, then the destination cell must be the candidate.

level 3: Limited candidates
If 2 cells of a cell set contain only 2 candidates in all, then these candidates can not appear in other cells within the same set. Same applies to 3 cells also.

level 4: Limited destination
If, within a cell set, a candidate only appear in 2 cells, then all other sets that contain these 2 cells can not have that candidate in all other cells. This can be generalized to 3 cells also

level 5: Dual line limited destination
For any 2 square sets on a common row, if a candidate only appear on 2 common rows of the 2 sets, then on the 3rd square set on the common row, the candidate can be eliminated from the 2 common rows.
Same applies to columns.

level 6: X-wing pattern
If there are two rows, where a candidate appears and only appears in the same 2 columns (the 4 cells form a rectangle), then we can be sure that the candidate appear in both columns at the one of the two rows. As a result, we can remove the candidate from other cells (excluding the intersections with the two rows) of the two columns.

level 7: Trial and error (Brute force search)
Just as it says.


FAQs

1. How do I play a puzzle?
You can play puzzle on the input tab. Just fill in the numbers. Once the puzzle is solved correctly, it will congratulate you. To fill in or change a cell, just move your mouse over the cell, and it will turn into a text cursor, allowing you to input numbers. If you put 1 number, it will assume the cell is known. If you put multiple numbers, it will assume they are candidates (make sure that View/Show candidates is checked). Move your mouse away from the cell to complete the input.


Notes

When View candidates is checked, the solver will take into account the candidate lists, and if the candidate lists are missing candidates, the solver will report unsolvable. This is designed to be a way to check whether your candidate lists are correct or not.

The Fix a conflict puzzle function may produce a fixed puzzle with multiple solutions. You can use the solver/Solve afterwards to make it single solution.

The Instant quick solvability check does not guarantee to detect a unsolvable puzzle. It is just a "quick check", not a "complete" check.
